Projects

My work spans disciplines from machine learning and mathematics to quantum computing, but nearly all of it converges on a single theme: cybersecurity. Whether directly security-focused or serving as a technological enabler, each project ultimately feeds back into strengthening and advancing the security landscape.

Quantum Password Strength Analyzer

A password strength analyzer program which can approximate how strong a password is against a quantum computer. Built using Python and Streamlit. Approximates the time taken to crack a given password via three methods.

DNSDino

DNSDino is a sleek, beginner-friendly OSINT web tool that helps you dig deep into any domain name. From WHOIS records to DNS resolution and IP geolocation, DNSDino provides a clean UI and actionable results. It's also a learning tool! It includes a comprehensive course on networks and DNS, with a total of 5 modules on different topics.

Pure Neural Network

A Neural Network built from the ground up using only Python and NumPy. Illustrates how neural networks operate under the hood while also demonstrating how high-level libraries abstract away much of the manual work.

Qubit Visualizer

An interactive Bloch sphere visualization tool I made for a seminar on Post-Quantum Cryptography. Used for exploring single-qubit quantum states. Apply quantum gates and watch your qubit evolve in real-time.

For a comprehensive overview of my tech stack and other smaller projects, refer to my GitHub.

About Me

I am an 18-year-old Computer Science student with a strong specialization in cybersecurity. I currently serve as an ISEA (Information Security Education and Awareness) Ambassador and as the Secretary of the OWASP Chapter at Sri Ramakrishna Engineering College.

My industry experience includes cybersecurity internships at Zyphron Technologies Pvt. Ltd. and Shadowfox, where I developed technical documentation, conducted Windows and web-based penetration-testing simulations, identified critical misconfigurations, and authored detailed exploitation and remediation reports.

I have delivered a technical talk on Quantum Computing and Cybersecurity to an audience of over 700 participants, and have presented a cybersecurity seminar for the Computer Society of India (CSI). I deliver talks frequently, and never turn down an opportunity to do so. I have also delivered multiple talks in my institution. I rank in the top 1% globally on TryHackMe, reflecting my continuous commitment to hands-on security training and skill development.

My current technical interests include Machine Learning for Intrusion Detection, DDoS attack classification, Post-Quantum Cryptography, and Red Team Operations. I am actively working on ML-based security applications.
